The big picture

What is Central Eurasia?

  • Lots of terms: Central Asia, Inner Asia, Central Eurasia, etc.
  • Even more definitions
  • Sometimes defined as:
    • the negative space between colonial enterprises
    • the "Silk Road" region
    • Post-Soviet "-stans"
    • any part of Asia that's not E, SE, S, (W), (N) Asia
  • None of these are necessarily "wrong"
  • I like to be fairly inclusive:
    • Japan to Finland, the Arctic to Persia—as relevant
    • At various points in history, each part of this area has been interconnected!

Ling 068: Structure of Kyrgyz

Core curriculum (linguistics)

studying all areas of the grammar of a single non-IE language

(phonetics, phonology, morphology, syntax, semantics, lexicon, etc.)

Additional curriculum (society, culture, history):

  • Activities like:
    • phonetic transcription of song lyrics,
    • learning to write Cyrillic and Perso-Arabic script
  • Presentations on non-linguistic topic of choice
    • Queer issues in Kyrgyzstan, Traditional sports in Kyrgyzstan,
      Religion in Kyrgyzstan, Music in Central Asia
  • Guest speakers
    (activists on Feminist & Queer art, Uyghur genocide, climate change in Central Asia, etc.)
  • Movie (usually Qurmanjan Datqa)
  • Cooking day (usually manty)
  • Presentations on another Turkic language
  • Final projects (+presentations)
    scaffolded independent research project on any connected topic

Ling 042: Voices of the Silk Road:
Languages, Societies, and Cultural Exchange in Central Asia

Core curriculum (linguistics)

introducing the study of palaeographically attested languages, language change, and language contact

Implementation:

  • Work directly with original texts in
    Ancient & Medieval Central Eurasian languages
    • Imperial Aramaic, Sogdian, Khotanese Saka, Tokharian B, Old Turkic, Middle Mongol, Tibetan
  • Explore historical linguistics topics via Central Eurasian examples
    • sound changes, linguistic reconstruction / the comparative method, autosegmental models of templatic morphology, language decipherment, isogloss mapping, linguistic convergence & divergence, tonogenesis, language relatedness
  • Student presentation of non-linguistic readings
    • Specific historical moments, snapshots of society; drawn in part from Whitfield (2015) - Life along the Silk Road, Hansen (2017) - The Silk Road A New History with Documents

Ling 073: Computational Linguistics

Overview of course

  • Students choose in small groups a traditionally excluded language to develop text-processing tools for over the semester
  • Lack of giant corpora → no machine learning
  • Developed tools include keyboard layouts, morphological analysis, morphological generation, lexical transfer, morphological disambiguation, lexical selection, structural transfer, machine translation

Central Eurasian component

  • Running example to demonstrate topics: Kyrgyz (also: Tuvan, Kazakh, Nivkh, Avar, and Spanish)
  • Result: students get exposed to a bunch of Kyrgyz
  • ... and it helps them learn the topic at hand

Conclusion

  • Students able to get quite a bit of Central Eurasian content
    ...by taking my classes
  • Can pair with other content in Asian Studies, Religion, Russian, Chinese, Japanese
    ...and get some real perspective
  • Lack of imagination?
    • No: students work on language of their choice in ling 073, lots of other languages in examples and problems in ling 045 & 052, ...
  • Selfishness?
    • A little bit maybe?
  • But it serves the students and the area of study
    • Perspectives that aren't mainstream
    • ... or even common in U.S. academia
    • Area of the world that I find endlessly interesting—and I try to share that enthusiasm with the students
